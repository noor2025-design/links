Life, History and Culture in Hong Kong

This collection explores the layered life, history, and culture of Hong Kong through moments that shape everyday experience. It brings together the infrastructures that move the city, including the history of trams and ferries and the constant rhythm of the city, which structure how people navigate urban life. Alongside these systems are long-standing cultural practices such as bamboo scaffolding and feng shui, reflecting deeply ingrained ways of building, organizing, and living in the city. The collection reflects everyday rituals and pauses within the city, from mahjong games and weekend hikes to the shared language of food. It confronts Hong Kong's social realities, including cage homes, the widening wealth gap, and scenes from recent protests that reveal ongoing tensions around identity, inequality, and belonging. Together, these materials present Hong Kong as a city defined by contrast, resilience, and the overlapping rhythms of movement, tradition, and daily survival.
